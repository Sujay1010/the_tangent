Big O notation is just anxiety with math notation
2 min read CS April 2026

Big O notation describes how an algorithm's runtime grows as the input gets larger. O(n) means it scales linearly. O(n²) means it scales quadratically. O(log n) means you're basically fine and should feel good about yourself.

But the way people talk about Big O in interviews has turned it into something else entirely. It's become a proxy for intelligence. A sorting algorithm — it's O(n log n), right? You hesitate. Anxiety. Suddenly you can't remember if merge sort is O(n log n) or O(n²) even though you knew this yesterday.

Big O is a tool for reasoning about scale. It's not a personality test. The anxiety we've attached to it is entirely invented and I think we should let it go. O(relax).
