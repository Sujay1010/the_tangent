Gradient descent is just vibes and we're all pretending
2 min read ML June 2026

Gradient descent is the engine behind pretty much every machine learning model you've ever heard of. And if you ask most people how it works, they'll say something about rolling a ball down a hill until it finds the lowest point.

Which is correct. And also deeply unsatisfying as an explanation for why it works on problems with millions of dimensions, highly non-convex loss surfaces, and no guarantee of finding a global minimum.

The honest answer is: it works because we tried it and it worked. The theory caught up later. Most of modern deep learning is vibes that got formalised after the fact. We're all just rolling balls down very complicated hills and hoping for the best. The hills happen to be in 175 billion dimensional space. But still. Vibes.
