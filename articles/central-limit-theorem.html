The Central Limit Theorem is the universe saying calm down
4 min read Statistics March 2026

The Central Limit Theorem says that if you take enough samples from any distribution — literally any distribution, no matter how weird — and average them, those averages will be normally distributed. Bell curve. Every time.

This is not obvious. This is not intuitive. The underlying distribution can be completely insane — bimodal, skewed, heavy-tailed, whatever. Take enough averages and the chaos organises itself into a bell curve.

The universe has a favourite shape and it will find a way to get there. I find this either deeply comforting or deeply suspicious depending on the day. Either the universe is telling us that things average out, or it's telling us that we've been tricked into thinking they do. The Central Limit Theorem doesn't tell you which one.
