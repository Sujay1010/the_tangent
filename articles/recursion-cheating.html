Why recursion feels like cheating but actually isn't
3 min read CS May 2026

The first time you see a recursive function, it feels like a scam. You're defining a function in terms of itself. That's circular. That shouldn't work. The whole thing should collapse into infinite regress and take the computer with it.

And yet it doesn't. Because of the base case — the point where the function stops calling itself and just returns an answer. The recursion unwinds. The stack resolves. You get your result.

Once you actually get it, you can't unsee it. Trees are recursive. Fractals are recursive. The way you understand a sentence — by understanding its clauses, each of which contains further clauses — is recursive. Your brain is doing recursion constantly and calling it thinking.

It's not cheating. It's just the shape that a lot of problems actually have.
