P vs NP is honestly society's problem
3 min read Math June 2026

P vs NP is the biggest unsolved problem in computer science. The question is whether every problem whose solution can be quickly verified can also be quickly solved. Most people assume the answer is no — that verifying is fundamentally easier than solving.

Nobody has proved it either way.

Here's what bothers me though. We've built most of modern civilisation on the assumption that P ≠ NP. Cryptography, security, digital signatures — all of it depends on the idea that certain problems are easy to verify but hard to solve. If someone proves P = NP tomorrow, everything breaks. Not metaphorically. Actually breaks.

We are collectively betting civilisation on a conjecture. That's not a maths problem. That's a society problem. Nobody talks about this enough.
