Every sorting algorithm is just trust issues in code form
3 min read CS April 2026

Bubble sort doesn't trust that anything is in the right place, so it checks every adjacent pair repeatedly until nothing moves. It's thorough. It's also extremely slow. Classic overcorrection from something that got burned once.

Quicksort picks a pivot, assumes everything smaller goes left and larger goes right, and recurses. It trusts its pivot. When the pivot is bad — when you pick the worst element every time — it collapses into O(n²) misery. Hubris, basically.

Merge sort doesn't trust anything to be sorted, splits everything in half, sorts each half independently, then merges. It never assumes. It verifies. It's the algorithm equivalent of 'I'll believe it when I see it.'

The algorithm you choose says something about your relationship with uncertainty. I think about this more than is probably healthy.
