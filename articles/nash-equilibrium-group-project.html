The Nash equilibrium hiding in your group project
9 min read Game Theory May 2026

A Nash equilibrium is a situation where no player can improve their outcome by changing their strategy, given what everyone else is doing. It's a concept from game theory that economists love and game designers obsess over.

It's also exactly what happens in every group project by week two.

The group project game

Here's the setup. Four people. Each person can either work hard or coast. If everyone works hard, the project is great. If everyone coasts, the project is mediocre. But if three people work hard and one coasts, the coaster gets the same good grade for less effort. So the rational move is to coast — as long as others work.

The tragedy isn't that people are lazy. It's that the incentive structure makes coasting the rational choice.

Why this matters beyond group projects

Nash equilibria show up everywhere people interact strategically — traffic, pricing, international relations, social media. Understanding them helps you see why perfectly rational people end up in collectively terrible situations all the time.
