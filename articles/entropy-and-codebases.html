How entropy explains everything wrong with your codebase
10 min read CS Theory May 2026

Entropy is one of those concepts that physics invented but forgot to warn everyone else about. Because it doesn't just apply to closed thermodynamic systems. It applies to your codebase. Your team. Your entire project.

The second law of thermodynamics says disorder in a closed system always increases over time. You cannot reverse it without adding energy from outside. Sound familiar?

Code is a closed system

Every codebase starts clean. Someone writes the first file with intention and care. Then a deadline hits. Then someone patches something they didn't fully understand. Then a library gets deprecated. Then two people solve the same problem differently and both solutions survive.

Entropy increases. The disorder compounds.

A refactor is just a temporary win against the second law. Entropy always gets the last word.

What this means for you

Messy code isn't a failure of discipline. It's physics. The question isn't how to prevent entropy — you can't. The question is how much energy you're willing to spend fighting it. Good software teams don't beat entropy. They just slow it down gracefully.
