Overfitting is just memorising the exam and failing life
2 min read ML March 2026

Overfitting is when a machine learning model performs brilliantly on its training data and terribly on everything else. It's learned the specific examples so well that it's lost the ability to generalise.

It memorised the answers. It didn't learn the material.

The parallel to education is so direct it hurts. Students who learn to game exam formats — who can pattern match past papers but can't apply anything to a new context — are overfitting. High training accuracy. Low real-world performance.

The solution in ML is regularisation — penalising the model for being too complex, forcing it to find simpler patterns that actually generalise. I'll let you draw your own conclusions about what the equivalent looks like in education.
