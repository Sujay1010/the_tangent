Why nobody actually teaches you the good stuff
3 min read Insight June 2026

So basically, in my opinion degree programs are speedrunners of human knowledge. Look at it this way. It took centuries of thought, experimentation from generations of researchers to build the modern aircraft. Da Vinci, Wright Brothers, Frank Whittle and the list goes on. Imagine learning the basics of aircraft in a 4 month semester with 2 assessments and a project with outrageous deadlines that you just ChatGPT your way out of it. And your professor... If I was one, I'd love to explain how SVM actually works with a billion parameters but with the 'Unit 3 by Friday' hanging above my head, I'd not.

And most important thing is the lack of interest that makes us ask: why? Teaching about the OLED TV while not calling out the backlit display of an LED-backlit TV. That's like showing Batman without his dead parents (forgive my humor).

The goal of us is not to become employable humans as directed by the system, but to become educated individuals. Ironically, the very idea of becoming those employable machines provides you a considerable disadvantage over the inquisitive individuals in the long run. And when this strikes in the heart of the system, prolly we may move towards better questions and when we move towards better questions we get better future.

Why only understanding a concept never works?

The interesting part of any subject is almost never in the formal curriculum. It lives in the question 'why?' - the reason a concept was invented, the failed attempts (like mine when I didn't know JS was needed for backend of this page), the test cases that break the 'once' perfect theory.

Take gradient descent. The textbook version is clean. Loss function, partial derivatives, step size, repeat. Elegant. Tidy. Completely useless for understanding why it actually works in practice. And here is the fun part, it actually doesn't work in practice. Every part relies on template and assumptions that don't hold in the real world. It's the continued practice to truly grasp it.

The Only Way to Truly Understand Anything

I prepared for my first car long drive by watching other's drive while sitting beside, youtube tutorials, driving sims in platforms and advices from my dad. And my first drive ended in a totally cool crash (yes whatever I do is cool). My second one was no better. But years later, I am a good driver and I can say that with honesty (I got my car grazed 30 days before writing this but who cares, this is my webpage). The actual point is PRACTICE. Yeah, I am bored to explain what practice is. So. Adios.

Knowledge is not an IKEA manual. Knowledge is having the capacity to build a new store.
